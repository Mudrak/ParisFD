Rapid Sequence Intubation

 

RSI is to be used in patients requiring sudden endotracheal intubation.  Some reasons for RSI include;   respiratory failure, asthma, croup, COPD, chest deformity, status epilepticus, head injury, maxofacial injury with airway compromise, spinal cord injuries, and burns.

 

Contraindications for RSI would include; Spontaneous breathing with good ventilation, major facial or laryngeal trauma, upper airway obstruction, distorted airway or facial anatomy, soft tissue penetrating injury to the neck, such as a gsw, stab wound with hematoma that compromises the airway.

 

Steps for RSI

  1. History and assessment of the patient to include what known allergies if possible.
  2. Administer O2, and establish an IV of normal saline KVO, EKG monitor
  3. Prepare the drugs that are necessary, (according to protocol), also get together necessary equipment, such as, pulse ox, laryngoscope
  4. Pre-oxygenate the patient with 100% oxygen.
  5. Possibly give premedication to the patient using such drugs as atropine in peds, (this is to keep the heart rate up), lidocaine in head injured patients, (to keep down swelling of the brain)
  6. Assist ventilations with use of cricoid pressure to prevent vomiting
  7. Sedate the patient using a sedative drug such as Versed.
  8. Give a neuromuscular agent to create muscle relaxation
  9. Intubate
  10. Verify placement of the tube by auscultation of the lungs, and visual, looking at the rise and fall of the chest and to assure that the stomach is not getting air.
  11. Ventilate and maintain inline stabilization for suspected c-spine patients.

 

Special points to consider;

 

Keep in touch with medical control when doing RSI.  You may consider a defasiculating dose of Vecuronium prior to administering Succinycholine.  Remember the ABC’s once the patient is paralyzed.  The patient will not be able to breath on their own and if intubation is unsuccessful, bag the patient with 100% O2, without air the patient will die.  You may also want to keep the patient sedated with continued use of Versed, per medical control.
